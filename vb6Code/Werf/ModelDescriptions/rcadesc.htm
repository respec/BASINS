General Description

RCA is Row-Column AESOP (Advanced Ecological Systems Modeling Program), a general modeling computer code. RCA was developed to interface with hydrodynamic models such as ECOM3D, EFDC and DELFT3D. RCA solves general mass balance equations for water quality variables of interest. The user is able, via the development of a FORTRAN subroutine, to tailor RCA to address the specific water quality issues of the water body under investigation. The FORTRAN subroutine prescribes the biological, chemical and/or physical kinetics or interrelationships between the relevant water quality variables of interest. Therefore, the user may run models from a simple conservative tracer, or first-order decay pathogen model to a more advanced eutrophication model coupled to a sediment nutrient flux model and a chemical equilibrium model.

RCA formulates mass balance equations for each model segment for each water quality constituent or state-variable of interest. These mass balance equations include all horizontal, lateral and vertical components of advective flow and dispersive mixing between model segments; physical, chemical and biological transformations between the water quality variables within a model segment; and point, non-point, fall-line and atmospheric inputs of the various water quality variables of interest.

Model methods include the following:

The partial differential equations which form the water quality model, together with their boundary conditions, are solved by finite difference techniques. The concentrations of the water quality variables are assumed to be centered within each water quality segment or grid cell.

The finite difference equations conserve mass. Two finite difference approximations are available for the space derivatives, central difference and backward difference. To reduce the numerical dispersion associated with backward difference solutions, RCA has been coded with an additional user selectable option that permits the use of a backward difference corrector scheme, based on Smolarkiewicz=s anti-diffusive velocity algorithm, which reduces the magnitude of the numerical dispersion in the finite difference solution. This procedure reduces numerical dispersion, but increases computational overhead.

RCA provides the user with a number of numerical schemes for solving the water quality mass balance equations. In time-variable mode, the user may utilize one of four explicit time-stepping algorithms, centered-in-time, centered-in-space, centered-in-time with a Smolarkiewicz corrector, and forward-in-time with or without a Smolarkiewicz corrector.

RCA grid size is taken from the hydrodynamic model used to drive RCA. RCA has been developed, so that it can be adapted to either a sigma level or Z-level segmentation in the vertical direction.

RCA permits the user two types of storage for computational results:(1) grid-wide dumps of all state-variables at Acoarse@ time intervals; and/or (2) detailed dumps for selected segments at Afine@ time intervals. Model output provides the user the ability to create both contour plots for any plane within the model and time series plots. The structure of the model output is defined in the kinetic subroutine.

Limitations

The user must be familiar with FORTRAN and have the source code to modify the systems available in the model. RCA currently does not have pre- or post-processing capabilities through a GUI.

Application History

Since its development in 1994, RCA has been used in numerous locations including Massachusetts and Cape Cod Bays, Jamaica Bay, the New York Bight, Green Bay, the Mississippi River, and Lake Victoria. Most applications have been with advanced eutrophication kinetics coupled to sediment nutrient flux, chemical equilibrium, and/or wetlands models.
